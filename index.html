MAKE THE PERFECT MATCHA

A ceremonial-style matcha is like espresso: a concentrated, premium product enjoyed in smaller quantities. As such, you only need about ½ teaspoon of powdered tea (two scoops from the chashaku) in order to make a single serving. Sift your matcha into a small bowl to remove any clumps. Then add two ounces of water at 176°F. Whisk to fully dissolve and incorporate the powder and to give you a creamy, velvety, and foam-topped cup. Once you have the basics down, you can adjust the amount of powder to your taste preference.


Matcha offers a range of health benefits due to its rich nutrient profile. It's packed with antioxidants, particularly catechins, which help neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. The high levels of L-theanine in matcha promote a state of calm focus, balancing the stimulating effects of caffeine, making it a great choice for sustained energy without the jitters. Additionally, matcha has been linked to improved metabolism and fat oxidation. It also supports cardiovascular health by helping to lower cholesterol levels and blood pressure. With its potential to enhance cognitive function, boost immunity, and provide a gentle detox, matcha is a holistic and energizing beverage choice.

To preserve the freshness and potency of matcha, proper storage is key. Here are some tips on how to store matcha effectively: Keep it airtight: Store matcha in an airtight container to protect it from air exposure, which can degrade its flavor and nutrients. A sealed tin or jar works best. Avoid light and heat: Matcha is sensitive to light and heat, which can cause it to lose its vibrant green color and antioxidants. Store it in a cool, dark place, away from direct sunlight or heat sources like stoves and ovens. Refrigeration (optional): While not strictly necessary, storing matcha in the refrigerator can help extend its freshness, especially if you live in a warm climate. Just be sure it's sealed tightly to prevent moisture from affecting the powder. Use it promptly: Matcha is best consumed within a few weeks to a couple of months after opening. Over time, it will lose its freshness, so try to use it quickly for the best flavor and nutritional benefits. By keeping matcha in a cool, dark, and airtight container, you can maintain its vibrant color, fresh flavor, and maximum health benefits.
